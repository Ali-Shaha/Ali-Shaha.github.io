This dissertation investigates the role of temperature stratification in the secondary combustion of gasoline surrogates within the end-gas of spark ignition (SI) engines. The study highlights the influence of the Negative Temperature Coefficient (NTC) on the prevalence of various combustion regimes within one and two-dimensional (1D/2D) scenarios.
